Complete Nigeria University Admission Guide 2026
Step-by-step from O'Level results to your university acceptance letter
1
Pass WAEC / NECO
Obtain minimum 5 credits in WAEC SSCE or NECO including English Language and Mathematics. Required alongside JAMB.
2
Register for JAMB
Buy e-PIN (₦5,500) at any bank or via *55019#. Register at jamb.gov.ng with your NIN and valid email.
3
Sit UTME Exam
Write CBT exam at an approved centre. Target 200+ for federal universities, 180+ for state universities.
4
Check Score & Match
Results in 24–48 hours. Use UniPredictNG to find universities matching both your JAMB score and O'Level.
5
Post-UTME Screening
Purchase your university's Post-UTME form (₦1,500–₦5,000) and sit the screening test. Prepare thoroughly.
6
Accept via CAPS
Monitor status at caps.jamb.gov.ng. Accept your offer, pay acceptance fees, and begin your university journey!

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions about Nigerian university admissions
Can I apply without WAEC results?

No. Every accredited Nigerian university requires O'Level results (WAEC SSCE, NECO or NABTEB) with minimum 5 credits including English Language and Mathematics. JAMB results alone are insufficient for any university admission.

What is the minimum JAMB score for university?

JAMB sets a minimum of 140 for universities. However, individual universities set higher cut-offs — most federal universities require 180–200 minimum, with competitive courses like Medicine requiring 250+. Private universities may accept from 140–160.

Can I use two sittings for WAEC?

Yes, for most courses. You can combine results from two different WAEC/NECO sittings to make up 5 credits. However, Medicine, Surgery, and Dentistry require all 5 relevant credits to be obtained in a single sitting.

What is catchment area and how does it affect admission?

Nigerian universities reserve a percentage of admission slots for students from their host state and neighboring states (catchment area). This can give local students an advantage, sometimes with lower cut-off scores. For example, UNILAG gives preference to Lagos, Ogun, and Oyo State students in some departments.

What is JAMB CAPS and how do I accept admission?

JAMB CAPS (Central Admissions Processing System) at caps.jamb.gov.ng is the official portal where universities send admission offers. Once offered, you must log in and click "Accept Admission" within the given deadline. After accepting, pay the school's acceptance fee within the deadline.

What is Direct Entry (DE) admission?

Direct Entry allows candidates with ND (National Diploma), NCE (National Certificate of Education), HND, or A-Level certificates to enter university at 200-level, bypassing 100-level. You must register for DE via JAMB, meet the university's DE requirements, and still possess O'Level results.
